Chicken Biriyani..

Description

Chicken Biryani is a flavorful and aromatic rice dish made with basmati rice, succulent chicken, and a blend of spices. Originating from the Indian subcontinent, this dish is known for its rich taste and layered cooking technique. The chicken is marinated in yogurt and spices like turmeric, cumin, coriander, and garam masala, then cooked with onions, tomatoes, and fragrant saffron-infused rice. The biryani is traditionally cooked using the dum method, where the ingredients are slow-cooked in a sealed pot, allowing the flavors to meld together. Garnished with fried onions, fresh coriander, and mint leaves, it is often served with raita (yogurt sauce) and salad. This dish is a perfect balance of spice, tenderness, and aroma, making it a beloved delicacy across various cultures.

--Stpes--

  1. Stpe 1: Marinade the chicken

    2. In a bowl, mix chicken, yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric, red chili powder, garam masala, coriander powder, cumin powder, salt, and lemon juice. Cover and let it marinate for at least 30 minutes (or overnight for better flavor).

  2. Stpe 2: Cook the rise

    3. Rinse and soak basmati rice for 30 minutes, then drain. In a pot, boil 4 cups of water with cardamom, bay leaf, cinnamon, cloves, and salt. Add the soaked rice and cook until 70-80% done (grains should still be firm). Drain the rice and set it aside.

  3. Step 3: Prepare the Chicken Masala

    4. Heat 2 tbsp oil or ghee in a large pan. Add sliced onions and sautÃ© until golden brown. Remove half for garnishing. Add cumin seeds, green chili, and chopped tomatoes; cook until soft. Add the marinated chicken and cook until it turns tender (about 10-15 minutes). Sprinkle garam masala, black pepper, and nutmeg powder. Stir well.

  4. Step 4: Layering the Biryani (Dum Cooking Method)

    5. In a heavy-bottomed pot, spread a layer of cooked chicken at the bottom. Add a layer of rice on top. Drizzle saffron-infused milk, fried onions, mint, and coriander leaves. Repeat the layers until all ingredients are used up. Drizzle ghee on top for extra flavor

  5. Step 5: Dum Cooking (Final Step)

    6. Cover the pot with a tight lid (or seal with dough for authentic dum cooking). Cook on low heat for 15-20 minutes to let the flavors blend. Remove from heat and let it rest for 5 minutes before serving.
